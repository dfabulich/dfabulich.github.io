Prologue

saving Dimwit from the evil witch

surviving Megaboz's attack

  1. Just DIVE UNDER A TABLE when everyone else does.

getting the scrap of parchment

  1. After Megaboz's departure, just get out from under the table. You can now pick up the scrap.

saving Dimwit from the evil witch

  1. Don't go on until you've read the enchanted fairy tale book.
  2. First you must get to the parapet area.
  3. Then say DIMWIT, DIMWIT, LET DOWN YOUR GOLDEN HAIR.
  4. There is no evil witch. There is no enchanted fairy tale book. And there is no reason that you should be reading this hint, unless you want to ruin the game for yourself by looking at hints you don't need!

opening the portcullis

  1. Ring the bell.
  2. Answer the jester's riddle!
  3. ANSWER "TIME"

the four torches

  1. Two of the torches are already burned out and the other two burn out within about 12 turns. Not too useful, eh?
  2. See the hints about finding a permanent light source in the General Questions section.

about the cauldron

  1. See the hints about the goal of the game in the General Questions section.

reading the rebus

  1. You'll have to remove those flattened animals.
  2. Something in the vicinity of the Gallery does something regarding the rebus.
  3. The key-shaped button in the closet. See the hints about these buttons n the General Questions section.
  4. You probably won't be able to remove all the animals until almost the very end of ZORK ZERO.
  5. Once you've removed all the animals, you should be able to decipher the rebus -- if you've noticed one key point.
  6. Look at the "N" in the upper left corner of the rebus.
  7. It's a mirror image of an "N." In fact, the entire rebus is a mirror image. Read it from right to left.
  8. The rebus, in text form, reads: (first line) TIE + BIRD - B + PIE + N (second line) EYE + URN + MIND - D (third line) W + OAR (fourth line) MATCH + CHICK + STORM - M
  9. This forms the words (first line) "tired pine" (second line) "iron mine" (third line) "wore" (fourth line) "magic store." Now you just have to figure out what to do with these words!

opening the walnut

  1. You can't open the nutshell without a tool.
  2. Namely, a nutcracker. Magic is also involved.
  3. Don't go on until you've found the magic wand and know what it does.
  4. The wand can turn something into a nutcracker.
  5. Point wand at lobster.

opening the door in East Hall

  1. You'll need a key from another section of the game.
  2. It's the iron key from the Solar (in the secret wing).

Encyclopedia Frobozzica

  1. It's extremely useful for reading about all sorts of interesting things. For example, try READ ABOUT MEGABOZ.

using the wand

  1. Have you read the writing on the wand?
  2. You can find out what the wand does by looking up "Fisha" in the encyclopedia.
  3. Try pointing the wand at various living creatures to see what they turn into.
  4. For example, the flamingo turns into a lawn ornament. Anything inanimated using the wand will return to its former state after 16 turns. Also, the wand can only be used 5 times, so don't waste it!

Tower of Bozbar

  1. To find out the rules, read about Tower of Bozbar in the encyclopedia.
  2. To move the tower to (for example) the right peg, you must get the 6-ugh weight to the right peg without crushing any other weight. Therefore, you must get the 5-ugh weight to the left peg without crushing other weights. And so on...
  3. Here are the keystrokes required to move the pile of weights to the left peg: 1R 2L 1L 3R 1C 2R 1R 4L 1L 2C 1C 3L 1R 2L 1L 5R 1C 2R 1R 3C 1L 2C 1C 4R 1R 2L 1L 3R 1C 2R 1R 6L 1L 2C 1C 3L 1R 2L 1L 4C 1C 2R 1R 3C 1L 2C 1C 5L 1R 2L 1L 3R 1C 2R 1R 4L 1L 2C 1C 3L 1R 2L 1L.
  4. When you "win," you'll be able to get to a new room by going north from the Jester's Quarters.
  5. There are three different Pyramid Rooms, depending on whether the six weights are on the left peg, the center peg, or the right peg.

large arched door

  1. It cannot be opened unless all six Tower of Bozbar weights are on the same peg. See the Tower of Bozbar hints in this section.

magic goggles

  1. Wear them.
  2. Try looking at various things.
  3. Try looking at a closed container with something inside, such as the package.
  4. The goggles give you x-ray vision.

opening the door in West Hall

  1. It's the steel key from the locker in the Garrison (in the bailey area).

riddle of the West Wing

  1. You can't go west from the West Wing until you've solved the jester's puzzle. You must physically show him something that satisfies his conditions.
  2. It's an object from a dark area of the castle.
  3. The walnut from the Root Cellar.
  4. You must open it in his presence (to prove that no one has seen it before) and then SHOW THE WALNUT TO THE JESTER.
  5. To prove that no one will ever see it again, simply EAT THE WALNUT.

winning Peggleboz

  1. Unfortunately, there's no good way to give hints for Peggleboz. There's no "trick" to it. Just keep trying, and if you finally give up, look at the next hint for one set of winning moves.
  2. Move the peg at Q to G. Then S to Q. H to R. U to L. I to K. T to I. E to J. O to G. A to E. F to N. M to O. L to J. P to F. F to C. B to D. D to K. Q to G. E to J. O to G. Other solutions are possible.

jester's shell game

  1. The jester is obviously pretty handy with magic, and is the sort of person who wouldn't be above cheating at a game like this.
  2. You can point at various shells all day long, but it won't get you the zorkmid bill!
  3. You must use magic yourself, and catch the jester cheating.
  4. You'll need a magical item from another area.
  5. It's something from the East Wing. Don't read on unless you've been there.
  6. The goggles from the Pyramid Room. Do you know what they do?
  7. They give you x-ray vision. If you go into the Gaming Room wearing them, you'll see where the zorkmid bill really is. Just point at that location.

flying the dirigible

  1. First, ENTER THE GONDOLA.
  2. Next, EXAMINE THE CONTROLS.
  3. Since you're in Flatheadia now, PUSH THE RIGHT BUTTON.
  4. Later, when you want to return from Fenshire, use the left button.

  2. You need a cleaning implement, which you'll find in a later section.
  3. The broom from winning Double Fanucci in Port Foozle.

Crypt

  1. See the hints about the funny paper in the Jester section.

Oracle

  1. Read about the Oracle of Bargth in the encyclopedia.
  2. The oracle can do amazing things -- if activated. See anything that might activate it?
  3. There's that depression on the forehead.
  4. You must put something in the depression.
  5. Something from the lake area.
  6. The moby ruby from the Lake Bottom.
  7. Once you've put the ruby in the depression, you can use the oracle. Don't expect it to talk to you though.
  8. ENTER THE ORACLE'S MOUTH. This will transport you to a new region!
  9. But wait -- there's more! Have you found the amulet?
  10. Notice any similarities between the amulet and the oracle?
  11. They're both serpent's heads with four eyes. Examine the amulet at several different times.
  12. Every four turns, the amulet will display a different number of open eyes.
  13. The oracle will transport you to a different region depending on how many eyes are open on the amulet.
  14. When no eyes are open, you'll go to the Crag. One eye --> Glacier. Two eyes --> Delta. Three eyes --> Foot of Statue. All four eyes --> Mine Entrance.

opening the drawbridge

  1. You can't open it from the Barbican...
  2. ...but you can from the Upper Barbican.
  3. TURN THE WHEEL. This will raise and lower the drawbridge.

opening the locker

  1. Try OPEN THE LOCKER.

Ursula Flathead

  1. Don't read any further until you've summoned Ursula using the ouija board.
  2. Examine Ursula's legs.
  3. Nice legs, huh?
  4. There's no ouija board and no way to summon Ursula Flathead. If you read hints about areas you're not stuck at, you'll ruin the game for yourself!

opening the doors at Perimeter Wall

  1. "It would take the power of a mighty wizard..."
  2. ...such as Megaboz.
  3. You won't be able to open these doors until you've stopped the Curse.

URS Office riddle

  1. Read it carefully.
  2. ANSWER "BOOKKEEPER"

pigeon and perch

  1. The address on the package should give you a clue.
  2. The pigeon is some kind of homing pigeon -- and the perch is presumably its "home."
  3. Have you noticed anything unusual about the behavior of the pigeon, such as when you pick it up.
  4. The address on the package refers to FrobozzCo Bldg, 193-E. If you go to the 193rd floor and go east, you'll find a clue -- but you should be able to figure it out without it.
  5. Try dropping both the pigeon and perch, and picking up the pigeon.
  6. Try dropping the perch in one room, the pigeon in another, and picking up the pigeon.
  7. The pigeon is a teleportation device. When a person, such as yourself, takes the pigeon, it teleports that person to the location of the perch. This can be quite useful.

climbing the FrobozzCo Building

  1. Since you have no rappelling equipment, you'd best do your climbing INSIDE the building.
  2. Have you found the memo on the fourth floor?
  3. Don't bother trying to find any of those teleportation tokens. The memo is just a "red herring." You'll have to climb the building on foot.
  4. Of course, other than your driving curiosity, there's no reason to go all the way to the top.

Testing Room booths

  2. Try dropping one (and only one) thing in one booth, getting in the other booth, and pushing the button.
  3. The above procedure turns you into whatever you put in the first booth. This is useful for solving a puzzle from another part of the game.

  2. When is a snake not a snake?
  3. Have you found the magic wand and used it on the snake?
  4. Once you've turned the snake into a rope, you can safely open the cage. The rope is useful elsewhere.

Construction site

  1. First, refer to the blueprint of this area that came in your Zork Zero package.
  2. You want to get to the room where Quizbo left his hardhat, but it isn't currently accessible.
  3. You can get a lot closer using the Frobozz Magic Passages.
  4. The closest you can get is room #17.
  5. Have you noticed any similarity between the Construction area geography and the geography of any other section of the game?
  6. Count the number of rooms in the Construction area. Don't go on until you've been somewhere else with the same number of rooms.
  7. The Plain. There's a connection between it and the Construction area. Read the hints about the Plain.
  8. Look at the relationship of room #17 and the hardhat room.
  9. You need someone's help...
  10. ...someone who can "jump" over the walls between you and the hardhat...
  11. ...such as the black knight or the white knight.
  12. You must figure out a way to get one of the knights to room #17.
  13. See the question about the pigeon and perch.
  14. Leave the perch in room #17, and give the pigeon to one of the knights. Return to room #17 by removing the cloak. The knight will have picked up the perch.
  15. Next, take the pigeon, and give the knight directions to send him to the hardhat room. Drop the pigeon, and pick it up again to teleport yourself to the hardhat room.
  16. Reverse the process to get the knight and yourself back to room #17. Take the hardhat from the knight, and wend your way back out of the Construction area. That's all, folks! There's no other purpose of the chess/Plain area besides supplying you with a knight!

giant sandcrab

  1. Use the tartar sauce.
  2. There's no giant sandcrab in ZORK ZERO.

Inquisition

  1. You need to go in there to get the box and its contents. Then of course, you need to get out alive.
  2. It's a logic puzzle. Read the rules, and think about their implication.
  3. When it's your turn, you need to ask the executioner one particular request.
  4. Upon entering, take a ticket. When your number is called, say EXECUTIONER, BEHEAD ME. (Don't forget to pick up the box while you're waiting.)

playing Double Fanucci

  1. Just play for a while, have fun, and try to deduce rules.
  2. For one thing, everything is consistent. For example, every time you single-play the Seven of Plungers, you will get the same result.
  3. So, if a particular play is illegal (the jester won't let you do it) that will always be true.
  4. There are, however, far too many rules for you to ever learn them all. Also, when either player exceeds a score of 1241 points, the game ends in a tie.
  5. You can cheat to your heart's content...
  6. ...but to win, you need an indefensible gambit.
  7. Sound familiar?
  8. Re-read your calendar.
  9. Specifically, the bio of Babe Flathead.
  10. Now that you know an indefensible gambit, wait for your opening and play it! (If any of the plays during your gambit are illegal, you're out of luck -- you'll just have to try again later.)

Room of Three Doors

  1. Read the sign in the Warning Room. Also read each of the three doors.
  2. This is just a straightforward logic puzzle. You must solve it every time you enter this room, because the signs on the doors are randomized each time.
  3. Make a list of all six possible combinations of doors: 1) Wishyfoo - Prevaricon - Veritassi 2) W - V - P 3) V - W - P 4) V - P - W 5) P - W - V 6) P - V - W
  4. Now, using the rules from the sign in the Warning Room, and the facts on each of the three doors, test the six combinations. Only one combination will fit all the rules.
  5. If you're still stuck, or want to save time in future visits to this area, here's a convenient rule: the door to Wishyfoo Territory is the one door whose sign mentions the Veritassi in statement #2.

royal yacht

  1. "You need nautical gear."
  2. Have you seen anything a sailor might wear?
  3. You must be wearing the seaman's cap from the Oubliette.
  4. Pushing each of the four colored buttons will send you to a different shore. Pushing the white button will send you to the center of the lake.

bathysphere

  1. You must be in it.
  2. You should close the door.
  3. PUSH THE LEVER DOWN to lower the bathysphere.
  4. The diving bell won't work when the yacht is docked at the shore, only when it's in the center of the lake.

that darn squid

  1. You won't be able to get that ruby until you deal with the squid.
  2. Don't look any further until you've explored Port Foozle.
  3. You need something from the Inquisition. See those hints.
  4. The pellet of squid repellent. Read the instructions on the box.
  5. You must get the pellet into the vicinity of the squid.
  6. You can drop it in the Hold...
  7. ...then go into the bathysphere and use the waldo to pick it up...
  8. ...then drop it when you get to Lake Bottom. You'll then be able to pick up the ruby in the waldo, unmolested.
  9. Alternately, you could throw the pellet off the yacht when you're in the center of the lake; it will sink to Lake Bottom.

Philharmonic Hall

  1. Something in the room's description is highlighted.
  2. Or, should we say, "spotlighted."
  3. The conductor's stand. STAND ON THE STAND.
  4. To get back from the Conductor's Pit, get off the stand and then stand on it again.

Theatre

  1. There's nothing important here.

crossing the bridge

  1. Try CROSS THE BRIDGE.
  2. There's no way to cross the stream. However, if you answer the jester's riddle here, you'll get the object that was on the other side of the stream.

riddle of the four men

  1. The men were not playing cards or any other kind of game. Reread the riddle with that in mind.
  2. Key words are "play" and "score."
  3. The men were musicians in a band. ANSWER "MUSIC."

riding the camel

  1. First, get on the camel.
  2. Then, just type the direction you want to move in, as you normally do.
  3. The camel will only enter the desert if "watered."
  4. Ride the camel to the Stream. He will now be willing to enter the desert. Once in the desert, you must locate the Great Underground Oasis, which will be useful later.

riding the unicorn

  1. First, get on the unicorn.
  2. It looks as if you can't ride a unicorn.

boulder

  1. Try PUSH THE BOULDER.
  2. If you don't want to sink the yacht, leave it docked at the South Shore when you push the boulder.

bowl of elixir

  1. You can't drink the elixir without a straw.
  2. There's a straw in the Kitchen, but if you've already tried, you know that it's deadly to drink the elixir. You want to do something else with the elixir.
  3. You simply want to touch the elixir.
  4. Since the bowl is so narrow, you can't reach the elixir -- at its current level.
  5. But there's a way to raise the level of the elixir.
  6. Have you ever gone DOWN from the Grotto?
  7. Put the gravel in the bowl.
  8. Each of the first three times you slide down the chute from the Grotto, you'll get a pile of gravel. If you put all three in the bowl, the level of the elixir will be raised enough to touch it.
  9. Now that you've touched the elixir, do you have a clue about what you've accomplished?
  10. The description you get from touching the elixir contains a clue. Also, look up St. Foobus in the encyclopedia.
  11. The elixir has given you "power over small insects."
  12. Such as flies.
  13. You can now take all the flies.

getting to the lower ledge

  1. The spire is important.
  2. You need to tie a rope to the spire.
  3. You won't find a rope anywhere...
  4. ...but you can "make" one...
  5. ...using magic.
  6. Have you found the magic wand? Read the hints about it.
  7. Turn the snake into a rope, tie the rope to the spire, and climb down. The snake will only stay a rope for 16 turns, so you have few turns to waste.

Enchanted Cave

  1. Read about Zilbeetha in the encyclopedia.
  2. You must find the orb in which Zilbeetha is imprisoned.
  3. You'll find four orbs beyond the toll gate.
  4. You must identify the correct orb or else (like many before you) you will die.
  5. There's a location somewhere in Zork Zero where the true essence of a magically-imbued item can be seen.
  6. It's somewhere "beyond" the oracle.
  7. It's the Mirror Lake below the Glacier.
  8. Look at each of the orbs in the mirror.
  9. Now return to the Enchanted Cave with Zilbeetha's orb and place it on the altar. The fragile flower is your prize.

hexagonal hole

  1. Most likely, when you're able to solve this puzzle, you'll know it.
  2. You must get a hexagonal block from somewhere in Fenshire.
  3. By solving the borphbelly stew puzzle.
  4. Put the hexagonal block into the hexagonal hole.

getting back to Flatheadia

Help! I'm lost

  1. There's a common method in interactive fiction for mapping maze-like regions. It's a way to make seemingly identical rooms non-identical.
  2. Drop a different object in each room. Then you'll be able to map the geography with no trouble!
  3. Delta locations will appear on the on-screen map only if you've dropped an object there.

toad and spyglass

  1. You must ask the toad for the spyglass.
  2. But he'll only allow himself to be addressed by his proper name. You must find out his name.
  3. Look to your packaging.
  4. More specifically, the calendar.
  5. More specifically, the biography on John Paul Flathead.
  6. Now that you know the toad's name, ask him for the spyglass.
  7. The flies which you've probably run across in various places are the Four Fantastic Flies of Famathria. See the hints in General Questions about the flies.
  8. Once you've gotten all four flies, give them to the toad and ask him again for the spyglass.

riddle of the lily pad

  1. Read the riddle again carefully, noting the various puns and allusions.
  2. The key words are "fool," "nonsense," and "wildebeest."
  3. There's no riddle of the lily pad. You shouldn't have even been looking at these hints!

Snarfem

  1. It's a math puzzle.
  2. The number of pebbles in each pile can be translated into a binary form: 0=0, 1=1, 2=10, 3=11, 4=100, 5=101, 6=110, 7=111, 8=1000, 9=1001. Then, add the four resulting numbers as though they were in base 10, not base 2. This sum should then be adjusted so that each digit is even (0, 2 or 4). Examine the four binary numbers, and determine which one must be reduced in order to create a sum whose digits are all even. Next, determine the number of pebbles you need to take from the appropriate pile in order to achieve the new binary number which will result in the all-even sum. That's your correct move for the current turn! For example, if you had piles of 7, 3, 9, and 2 pebbles, the binary equivalents would be 111, 11, 1001, and 10. The non-binary sum of those four numbers is 1133. If 1001 were reduced to 110, the new sum would be 242 -- all digits even! In order to change 1001 (binary 9) to 110 (binary 6), remove 3 pebbles from pile 3. It's that simple!
  3. Some of you may be just a tad confused. Take heart! There's also a non-mathematical solution!
  4. As you play, watch the screen carefully.
  5. Specifically, keep an eye on the blossoms.
  6. Notice how they are constantly opening and closing. Notice any pattern?
  7. How many flowers are on each side?
  8. The four flowers on the left side are telling you (or the jester) which pile to select. The nine flowers on the right side indicate how many pebbles to remove from that pile.
  9. If there's no "correct" move to make, all the blossoms will be closed. However, since you get to go first, if you keep making the "correct" move every turn, it will be the jester who will never have a "correct" move, and you will win every time!

  3. If the jester has crossed the quicksand, you've got to bring the ingredients to him. But you can only carry one thing at a time across the rocks. Worse, the fox will eat the rooster if you leave them alone, and the rooster will eat the worm if you leave them alone.
  4. Drop everything but the rooster. Cross the quicksand. Drop the rooster. Go back and get the fox. Cross the quicksand. Drop the fox and get the rooster...
  5. Go back, drop the rooster, and get the worm. Cross the quicksand and drop the worm. Finally, go back and get the rooster!

  1. Riddle hints are in the sections relating to the location where you're asked the riddle.

killing the jester

  1. You can't. There's no way to get the jester to leave you alone. In fact, you wouldn't want him to -- he does some useful things as well as annoying things.

scroll

  1. The scroll is a word puzzle.
  2. Try rearranging the letters in "RAM A NAG."
  3. They rearrange to "ANAGRAM," of which this message is an example.
  4. The scroll anagrams to "ENTER LIBRARY; TOUCH ARMOR THRICE." So -- do so!

slate

  1. The first step is finding the tree in question. The dusty old slate refers to the tree as the oldest elm around.
  2. Examine the slate.
  3. The slate itself is very old!
  4. So, you're looking for what was the oldest elm tree around...a century ago!
  5. The elm in question has since died...
  6. ...and is now the stump in the Outer Bailey.
  7. Stand on the stump and follow the instructions on the slate (these directions vary each time you play). You've now identified a spot.
  8. But, as you can tell by the fact that nothing happened, you're not done yet. Do these directions remind you of any sort of classic directions?
  9. They're just like the directions you might expect to find on a map leading to buried treasure!
  10. Once you've obtained the shovel from Wishyfoo Territory, follow the slate directions from the stump and dig a hole in the ground.

that pesky bedbug

  1. Re-read the jester's parting comment for a clue.
  2. Also, examine the bedbug.
  3. The bedbug is very, very tired.
  4. You need to put it to sleep.
  5. How would you normally put someone or something to sleep? Like a child, for instance...
  6. Try singing a lullaby, or yawning, or lying down, or going to sleep yourself...
  7. Alternately, you could use the wand, but that would waste precious wand shots.

funny paper

  1. Read it.
  2. Once you've gotten to the Crypt, look under the slab mentioned in the funny paper! (The slab in question changes each time you play.)

permanent light source

goal of the game

  1. The key to stopping the Curse is contained on the scrap of parchment which fell out of Megaboz's pocket (and which is enclosed in your Zork Zero package). Read it carefully.
  2. Only the long piece of writing toward the left-center of the scrap is important -- but some of it is cut off.
  3. This missing text is:
    The Curse cannot...
    Except by...
    To each...
    Tossing it...
    Then...
  4. Good luck, Blackbeard.

all those key-shaped buttons

  1. Try pushing them, of course!
  2. The first time you push a button, it produces a sound. Do these sounds remind you of anything?
  3. The sounds are like the sounds made by certain animals...
  4. ...specifically, the animals covering the rebus!
  5. Each time you push a button, one of the rebus animals will go away. There are six buttons, corresponding to the six animals, located in the Closet, the FrobozzCo Basement, the Grotto, the Crag, the Crawl, and the Attic.

getting the flies

  1. You can't catch them, even with the added help of the Frobozz Magic Glove.
  2. You need to do something magical before you can get the flies. You'll probably know it when you come to it.
  3. See the hints about the elixir in the Lake Area section.
  4. The flies are in the Parapet (Secret Wing), the Great Underground Savannah (Lake Area), River's End (Delta Area), and inside the trunk in Megaboz's Attic.
