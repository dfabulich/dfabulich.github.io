How do I get into the house?

  2. Get Holmes's mind off the drug.

Can I get Holmes out of his stupor?

  1. Holmes is bored with the commonplace events of everyday life.
  2. He needs some mental excitement.
  3. Newspapers generally contain interesting items.
  4. For a doctor, you're not too bright. Read him the newspaper. (Or tell him about the visitor).

Why does the clue paper exist?

  1. It looks like the thief is challenging Sherlock Holmes to a duel of wits.
  2. Unlike yourself, Sherlock is not unarmed for such a duel.
  3. The thief is laying down a trail of clues for Holmes to follow.

What's the answer to the 1st riddle?

  1. The verse refers to a particular place.
  2. The place is the spot where most English royalty is buried.
  3. Westminster Abbey.

What's the answer to the 2nd riddle?

  1. The verse refers to the last of the Tudor monarchs.
  2. She was called the "Virgin Queen."
  3. Elizabeth I.

What's the answer to the 3rd riddle?

  1. The discoverer of the law of gravity.
  2. Sir Isaac Newton.

What's the answer to the 4th riddle?

  1. Don't read the next clue until you have thoroughly explored Westminster Abbey.
  2. Have you looked around in the Jerusalem Chamber?
  3. The fourth verse refers to Henry V.

Where is the lamp?

  1. It's in Holmes's bedroom.

Who is the thief?

  1. Who do you think?

Are the tombs important?

  1. They are to the people buried in them.
  2. Yes.

How do I make a brass rubbing?

  1. Take a piece of paper from the pacquet of paper, put it on the tomb of your choice, and then rub it with the crayon.

What should I make rubbings of?

  1. Do not read the next clue until you have solved the verses on the thief's clue paper.
  2. The tombs of Queen Elizabeth, Sir Isaac Newton and Henry V.

Why am I making brass rubbings?

  1. The thief is trying to send messages to Sherlock.
  2. He wouldn't want his messages to be visible to everyone, now would he?
  3. Have you visited the British Museum yet?
  4. The book on the table in the British Museum contains a clue that will help you.
  5. There is some invisible writing on some of the tombs.
  6. When you make a rubbing, the invisible ink is transferred onto the back of the paper.
  7. Invisible ink usually becomes visible when heated.
  8. Heat the paper over the candles in the Evangelist Chapel.
  9. Turn the paper over.

Where is the Stone of Scone?

  1. It belongs under the Coronation Chair in Edward the Confessor's Chapel - but we knew it was going to be stolen in 1950 and then returned several months later. We figured that was enough excitement for any one rock, so we left it out of the game, safe from your grubby paws.

How do I stop rowing in circles?

  1. Has anyone told you you're not playing with a full deck?
  2. Has anyone told you you're not rowing with both oars in the water?
  3. You need two oars to steer the boat.
  4. Don't read the next two clues unless you want to know where the other oar is.
  5. The other oar is in another boat.
  6. It's in Captain Bligh's boat in the Black Museum.

How do I stop the boat?

  1. Type, 'Whoa, Boat!'
  2. Just kidding. Drop the anchor.

How do I launch the boat?

  1. Got any rocket fuel?
  2. Type, 'Launch boat.'
  3. (Make sure you're in the boat and the anchor's up first.)

How do I land the boat?

  1. You can only land the boat at the Embankment or outside Traitor's Gate.
  2. Type: N or LAND BOAT.

How do I get the paddle?

  1. You can't get the paddle when you're outside Traitor's Gate.
  2. If you don't have the paddle, you need two oars to row back up the river.
  3. If you're on the river without a paddle or both oars, you're making a big mistake.
  4. You can only take the paddle after you've entered the Tower.

Can I stop the man taking the boat?

  1. No, you can't. But after all, it's his boat.

What is the matter with the girl?

  1. She has a bad heart.
  2. Perhaps some medicine....
  3. Be careful. Drugs are dangerous.

How do I revive her?

  1. Have you made a positive diagnosis?
  2. Listen to the girl with your stethoscope.
  3. If her heart is abnormally fast, give her something to slow it down.
  4. An unusually fast heartbeat is known as tachycardia.
  5. If her heart is abnormally slow, give her something to speed it up.
  6. An unusually slow heartbeat is know as bradycardia.
  7. Belladonna will speed up a slow heart.
  8. Digitalis will slow down a fast heart.
  9. The drugs are in your medicine bag.

Where is the stethoscope?

  1. Where do most doctors carry their stethoscopes?
  2. You are not most doctors.
  3. You have it when you start the game.
  4. Sorry about this. It turns out that Watson always carries his stethoscope in his hat. Really. (Read "A Scandal in Bohemia.")

Why do I finish without 100 points?

  1. If you play the game from start to finish and solve all the puzzles, you will end up with 100 points. If, however, you start the game and play through it using information you acquired in a previous session, you may have neglected to perform a few actions for which you received points. This should not be of concern to anyone who is content with failing to discover the highest ranking available to the game player.

Can I abbreviate "stethoscope"?

  1. Yes. It can be abbreviated to STETH or SS.

What is the square root of 64?

  1. Eight.
