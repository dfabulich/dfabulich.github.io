How do I climb the endless stair?

  1. If you wish to go back to Zork II, just switch diskettes!

How do I get the sword in the stone?

  1. "Who do you think you are? Arthur?"
  2. "The sword is deeply imbedded within the rock. You can't even begin to budge it."
  3. Be patient. You'll get it when the need for it arises.

How can the runes be translated?

  1. A Runish-to-English dictionary?
  2. Have you examined them?
  3. Beyond that, you'll get nothing more.

How do I climb the channel in the Damp Passage?

  1. "The channel climbs too rapidly and the moss is slippery."

What will increase traction?

  1. Studded snow tires work well on glare ice.
  2. Some purists insist on chains.
  3. If you were thinking of climbing the slippery moss, forget it. You really can't go that way.

How can I clear the rubble in the Damp Passage?

  1. "The channel opening is blocked by tons of debris."
  2. You can't.

Where do I find the old and wizened man?

  1. The runes are a very subtle hint.
  2. There is a chance he will appear when you enter (and only when you enter) the Engravings Room.

How do I waken the old and wizened man?

  1. He is sleeping very soundly. (Listen to that snore.) Try saying WAKE MAN or SHAKE MAN.

What do I do about the old and wizened man?

  1. Attacking him would not be very sporting, but it might give you a clue.
  2. He is described as "weak," "tired," and "frail."
  3. Have you considered helping him? You might give him something he could use.
  4. Try giving him the waybread.

Can the phosphorescent moss be kept alive for more than 10 moves?

  1. Certainly. It will live throughout the entire game.
  2. But you will never be able to take it, move it, or use it as a light source anywhere else.

How can I open the Great Iron Door?

  1. You cannot open the door.
  2. However, after the earthquake you will find a cleft in the rock that will allow you to pass.

How do I get the rust remover tube from the warlock?

  1. What a looney!

How do I get out of the chasm?

  1. How did you get down to the chasm?
  2. The folks at Infocom would be very interested.

I'm trapped on the Aqueduct. What do I do?

  1. If you can't proceed to the north due to a collapsed segment, you're stuck.
  2. What might have caused this segment to collapse?
  3. The secret is to traverse this area before it is destroyed by the earthquake.

Can the lake be crossed?

  1. "Go jump in a lake!"
  2. Seriously! Jump in and swim to one of the other shores.

What is required to walk on water?

  1. A messianic complex?
  2. Do you also want to move mountains?

How do I catch the fish?

  1. Just be careful that it doesn't catch you!

What is a Roc?

  1. It is a legendary bird of prey of tremendous size and strength. Your probability of seeing it is low, but if you do, watch out!

What can be done with the swamp gas?

  1. Swamp gas burns...
  2. If you could transport it across the lake, it might make a good light source.

How do I transport the swamp gas?

  1. A bottle would be useful.
  2. Unfortunately, there are no bottles. By the way, where did you find swamp gas?
  3. Remember the introduction? If every question in this book were pertinent to the game, the questions themselves would give far too much away. Do not use the presence or absence of a question on a certain topic as an indication of what is important, and don't assume that long answers are associated with important questions.

How can I get the shiny object?

  1. Try to take it until you eventually succeed. (There is a 50% probability that you will get it on any move. Although there is less than one chance in a thousand that you wouldn't get it within 10 tries, it could conceivably take zillions of tries.)

What is the table with the "Scenic Vista" sign for?

  1. Have you ever examined it?
  2. Have you ever touched or rubbed it?
  3. You can look at or go to sections of Zork I, Zork II, Zork III, and Enchanter.

Is there anything under the manhole cover?

  1. Try raising or moving it.

How do I get a light source to the southern end of the lake?

  1. The light sources cannot be relit once they've gotten wet.
  2. They can't be taken across the lake without getting wet.
  3. There is no way to get a working light source to the south shore of the lake.
  4. There is another way!
  5. Have you seen the grue repellent?
  6. Figure out how to use the viewing table.
  7. Use the grue repellent to get to the Key Room and Aqueduct. The viewing table is also useful for pre-positioning the light source you will eventually need.

How can I get to the coal in the Dead End?

  1. You can't.
  2. You are limited to three moves in the areas visited with the viewing table. There is no way for you to increase this. Obviously, if you could stay as long as you wanted, there would have to be four games on the diskette.

What light source can I take to the west of the Timber Room?

  1. None.
  2. See question above.

What do I do about the hooded figure?

  1. Have you noticed anything you do which increases your score?
  2. You must fight the figure.
  3. Have you ever looked at the figure or looked under the hood?
  4. When badly injured, the figure is defenseless.
  5. Does killing the figure leave you feeling satisfied?
  6. While the figure is badly injured, try taking the hood.

Why do I see myself?

  1. Very good question.
  2. This is actually a hint.

How do I freeze the quicksand?

  1. A blast of supercold air might do the trick.
  2. The Great Underground Empire is far from polar regions.
  3. The quicksand can't be crossed. It is one of the many borders which keep the Underground Empire small enough to fit on a diskette.

How do I get to the ship?

  1. Congratulations. Seeing the ship is rather rare.
  2. Have you tried walking on water?
  3. This is a Viking sailor.
  4. You've been waiting three games for this!
  5. Something happens here!
  6. Say HELLO SAILOR!!

How do I open the chest?

  1. You can't open it.
  2. The man at the cliff has the only key.
  3. You'll have to wait for him to open it.
  4. Wait on the ledge for him to appear and follow his directions.

Can the man at the cliff be trusted?

  1. He certainly seems like a practical joker.
  2. But he has the only key to the chest.
  3. Do exactly what he says -- and be patient.

What is in the tree house?

  1. This could be the man's living quarters.
  2. The inflatable raft in the back room might be useful elsewhere.
  3. It is amazing that you got this far when there is no tree house.

I have the treasure, but how do I mend the staff?

  1. It cannot be fixed.
  2. Have you tried protecting the staff (for example, throwing it off the cliff) before taking the treasure?
  3. You'll have to decide which you prefer, the treasure or the staff.

I have the staff; how do I get the treasure?

  1. You might try killing the man before he walks away with it.
  2. You'll have to decide which you prefer, the treasure or the staff.

What is the significance of Smokey the Bear?

  1. "Only you can prevent forest fires."
  2. Maybe you thought this was one of the nonsense questions. Try burning the trees.

What is in the vial?

  1. "It feels as though there is something inside, but you can't see anything even though the vial is transparent."
  2. Have you tried drinking the contents?
  3. Look into the mirror immediately after drinking it.
  4. The invisible liquid is an invisibility potion. It will last only two moves and will not be effective against the more powerful denizens of the Great Underground Empire.

When are the museum personnel on duty?

  1. Intrepid adventurers do not fear mere warning notes.
  2. They all died years ago. You'll have to risk it on your own.

Is there anything I can do when I'm trapped in the small square room with marble and sandstone walls?

  1. There is a way out.
  2. Sandstone is a very light rock.
  3. Try pushing one of the sandstone walls (PUSH EAST WALL or PUSH SOUTH WALL).

Where do I find dynamite?

  1. What a strange concept.
  2. This space intentionally left blank.

What are the ladders for?

  1. Remember the hole through which you entered the puzzle?
  2. It is possible to maneuver one of the ladders into a position allowing you to climb out.

What is the slot for?

  1. Have you tried putting anything in it?
  2. Try the lore book.
  3. This is one (although not the best) way out of the puzzle.

I'm lost in the puzzle. How do I get out?

  1. There are ways out.
  2. Try mapping (movable pieces to represent walls may help). Push as few walls as possible.
  3. You might make use of the slot (not recommended).
  4. Walls can only be pushed, never pulled.
  5. Starting from the Royal Puzzle entrance, the following moves will solve the puzzle.
    D. PUSH EAST WALL. S. S. SE. PUSH SOUTH WALL. N. NE. PUSH SOUTH WALL. TAKE BOOK. PUSH SOUTH WALL. E. NE. PUSH WEST WALL. SW. NW. NE. PUSH SOUTH WALL. SW. PUSH EAST WALL. NE. PUSH SOUTH WALL. NW. N. N. PUSH EAST WALL. SW. S. SE. NE. N. PUSH WEST WALL. NW. PUSH SOUTH WALL. AGAIN. W. NW. NW. PUSH SOUTH WALL. SE. SE. SE. NE. PUSH WEST WALL. AGAIN. SW. PUSH NORTH WALL. AGAIN. AGAIN. NW. UP. YELL. (Note: Innumerable other sequences will reach the same result.)

What was the "snap" I heard?

  1. You ran into something with the wall. Movable objects are transported to the side room when hit by the wall.

What does the gray machine do?

  1. This is a pressurizer.
  2. It is like the machine used in Zork I to make diamonds.
  3. Unfortunately, it is not a working model.

What does pushing the button on the gold machine do?

  1. Nothing if you aren't sitting on the seat when you push it.
  2. Nothing if you haven't changed the setting of the dial before pushing the button.
  3. This is a time machine. Pushing the button transports the person sitting in the chair (and only that person -- the time machine itself doesn't move) to the same spot in the year indicated on the dial (where you may find the time machine in the same position). The effect is not permanent -- in 40 moves you will snap back. The present year is 948.

What does the black machine do?

  1. This is a room spinner.
  2. Presumably it is a model of the machine which spins the Carousel Room in Zork II.
  3. Fortunately, it is not a working model.

How can I read the plaque in the Technology Museum?

  1. It has faded with age and can no longer be read.

How do I unlock the cage?

  1. That cage will protect the royal jewels until the end of time.
  2. It is, however, possible to get your hands on the jewels.
  3. You should learn the use of each machine in the Technology Museum.

How do I take the royal jewels?

  1. The cage is impregnable, but you can't let small details like that stop you. Read the items to unlocking the cage before continuing.
  2. By now you know that the gold machine is a time machine which can be used to go to the Jewel Room before the cage is installed. (Push the machine to the Jewel Room in the present and go back to the year 776.) Unfortunately, you can't carry anything with you through time.
  3. You must find a very good hiding place. Lord Dimwit Flathead's men will no doubt launch an extensive search for the missing jewels. They must remain hidden for 200 years.
  4. You might check the machines for a good place. (Wait for the guards to leave.)
  5. Have you looked under the seat in the time machine?
  6. Excessive greed in an adventurer of your status is unbecoming.
  7. Only the ring can be successfully hidden.
  8. If you succeed in getting the ring, Flathead has the other jewels moved. If you botch the burglary, two things can happen: 1) They realize the time machine works and remove it; or 2) They find the jewels displaced and simply put them back (but a different plaque is installed).

What do I do about the robot?

  1. Congratulations. It is a very rare treat to see the robot. Just try again.

What does the red button do?

  1. Pushing the button alone is not notably helpful.
  2. Pushing the button is helpful if another action precedes it.
  3. Haven't you been curious about the red beam of light?
  4. Block the beam with an object, then push the button.
  5. You will find that the mirror is a doorway.

What do I do about the red beam of light?

  1. You can jump over it, but it won't do you much good.
  2. Trying to take it is similarly useless.
  3. Does the fact that it is only an inch above the floor suggest anything?
  4. Have you tried blocking it with your hand?
  5. Try blocking it with an object. You will now find that the button is useful.

Is the mirror important?

  1. Presumably you have already tried touching it, looking into it, pushing it, breaking it.
  2. It is important, but you will have no direct interaction with it.

  2. You don't.

What is the significance of the different colored walls?

  1. By making them different colors, you can refer to them separately.
  2. Pushing on the red or yellow walls can cause the box to rotate on the central pivot pole in a clockwise direction. Pushing the black or white walls can cause the box to rotate counterclockwise.

What is the significance of the pine wall?

  1. If nothing is blocking it on the other side, pushing on it will cause it to swing out so that you can leave the box.

What is the significance of the mahogany wall?

  1. If the box is aligned properly, pushing on it will cause the entire box to move.

What is the long pole for?

  1. The long pole is at the center of the box and extends from the ceiling to the floor.
  2. It is a pivot around which the box can be turned.

What is the short pole for?

  1. Doesn't the hand grip suggest anything?
  2. "The pole has been dropped into a hole in the stone floor."
  3. Try raising the short pole.
  4. The short pole anchors the box in position.
  5. It can also be used to steady it when it is moved.

What is the T-bar for?

  1. It has an arrow carved on it.

What is the significance of the direction the arrow is pointing?

  1. That is the direction the mahogany wall faces.

Is the wobble significant?

  1. It can be avoided.
  2. It can also have negative consequences.
  3. If you drop the short pole into the channel, it will steady the movement of the box.

How can I undress the stone?

  1. Pervert!
  2. Look up "dressed" (as it applies to stone) in the dictionary.

Why am I being killed while inside the box?

  1. Once you have rotated the box 90 [degrees] from its original position, you may want to leave it to scout the area beyond.
  2. Learn all you can about the Guardians of Zork.
  3. If the Guardians notice the box due to a broken mirror, open door, or a wobble, they will kill you.

How can I pass the Guardians of Zork?

  1. The Guardians strike anything which they see between them.
  2. The Guardians are described as "identical" and "perfectly symmetrical."
  3. If you walk in front of them or throw something in front of them, they destroy "in perfect unison."
  4. Do you think a Guardian could distinguish between the sight of the other and the sight of himself in a mirror?
  5. You can travel past them inside the mirror box. ("It's all done with mirrors.")
  6. An alternate solution is to walk past while invisible.

How is the scuba tank attached to the wet suit?

  1. What scuba tank and wet suit?
  2. This space intentionally left blank.

I seem to have gotten past the Guardians, but I'm still being killed. What should I do?

  1. Rotate the structure so that the door doesn't open into their field of view.

How do I open the large wooden door with the barred panel?

  1. You can't open it.
  2. Have you tried knocking?

What must I do to satisfy the old man at the large door?

  1. "He looks you over with his keen, piercing gaze and then speaks gravely..."
  2. This is the Dungeon Master.
  3. He is looking for something. What you think might be valuable may have nothing to do with it.
  4. Most of the problems in the game are tests by the Dungeon Master to see whether you are ready.
  5. You have met him before when you have died.
  6. Does nothing about his description strike you as odd?
  7. "He is dressed simply in a hood and cloak, wearing a few simple jewels, carrying something under one arm, and leaning on a wooden staff. A single key, as if to a massive prison cell, hangs from his belt."
  8. You can acquire his entire costume by solving the problems in the game.
  9. Your goal is to become the Dungeon Master. You will need the hood and cloak, the amulet and ring, the key, the wooden staff, and the lore book.

Why won't the Dungeon Master follow me?

  1. Have you asked him to?
  2. Say DUNGEON MASTER, FOLLOW ME. (See your game manual.)

What should be thrown into the flaming pit?

  1. The end had come, and this was it;
    He dropped her in the flaming pit.
    -- Edward Gorey
  2. Anything dropped into the pit is lost forever.

What will scratch the marble?

  1. Huh?

What are the sundial and button for?

  1. You can set the dial to any of the eight numbers.
  2. Try spinning or setting it and then pushing the button. Did anything change?
  3. You might try putting something in the cell first.
  4. Reading the lore book now might help.
  5. This is a magic dungeon (no doubt a product of Frobozzco). There are eight cells. Pushing the button causes the cell selected to appear, and the cell which was there to go back to its usual spot.

How do I unlock the bronze door?

  1. Have you tried the key?
  2. This is the first lock it has fit. Why do you think it won't turn?
  3. You must learn more about how this dungeon works.
  4. It will unlock only if you are in cell 4 and in the cell's normal location. While in cell 4 type: DUNGEON MASTER, TURN THE DIAL TO 8 THEN PRESS THE BUTTON.
  5. The bronze door will now be unlockable with the key.

How do I get out of the cell?

  1. Can't you open the door?
  2. If the dial has been set to a new number and you have had the Dungeon Master push the button while you were in the cell, there is no way back.

What is my goal? What am I supposed to be doing?

  1. When you come to the entrance to the dungeon and meet the Dungeon Master, your purpose may become clearer.

How does the scoring in this game work?

  1. The scoring is a hint as to what is important.
  2. The points are not "earned" by solving problems or acquiring items. You receive a point when you start on a path where you have a potential for progress in the game.
  3. It is possible to have all seven points without correctly solving any of the problems.

How do I relight the burned-out torch?

  1. I see no matches here.
  2. There is no way to relight the torch. You'll have to find a way to avoid getting it wet.

How can I repair the lamp?

  1. If it has gotten wet or the battery has run out, the lamp is out for good. It is always best to conserve resources. You can prolong the life of the lamp by keeping it dry and turning it off whenever alternate light sources are available.

How Points Are Earned

(Use only as a last resort.)

You earn one point when you...
  • encounter the hooded figure.
  • attack the hooded figure.
  • reach the Cliff Ledge.
  • jump in the lake.
  • touch the viewing table.
  • push the button on the time machine while the dial is set to 776.
  • push a sandstone wall in the Royal Puzzle.
  • Of course, you can have all seven points and still be far from completing the game.
