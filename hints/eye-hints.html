Walkthrough / Gradual Hints for EYE by Arthur DiBianca

These hints are designed to give you just the hint you need to get to the next part.

General advice

  • Take notes on everything you find.
  • Wordplay is sometimes required.

Getting started: "You have arrived."

  1. The first puzzle of the game is to figure out what to type.
  2. "[Error: invalid length]" is a hint about what to type.

  6. In the hints below, I'll tell you what each kind of word is.

    Neigborly

    1. In a Neighborly word, all letters are one step away from another letter, e.g. ABC.

    Extreme

    1. Extreme is kinda the opposite of neighborly.
    2. Extreme words contain the letter A.
    3. Extreme words contain the letter A and the letter Z.

    Balanced

    1. Many Neighborly words are Balanced.
    2. Balanced words are like a balancing scale, with the same weight in each balancing pan.
    3. If a Neighborly word is Balanced, how would you make a Balanced word that isn't Neighborly?
    4. In a Balanced word, the distance from the first letter to the middle letter is the same as the distance from the middle letter to the last letter.

    Early

    1. In an Early word, all of the letters have something in common.
    2. All of the letters of an Early word are Early.
    3. In an Early word, all of the letters are before the letter K (not including K).

    Late

    1. In a Late word, all of the letters have something in common.
    2. All of the letters of a Late word are Late.
    3. In a Late word, all of the letters are after the letter R (not including R).

    Difficult

    1. In a Difficult word, all of the letters have something in common. (All of the letters in a Difficult word are Difficult.)
    2. There are only about a dozen words in the Scrabble dictionary that are Difficult.
    3. Most of the letters in the alphabet are Difficult.
    4. A Difficult word contains no vowels AEIOU or Y.

    Vocalic

    1. In a Vocalic word, all of the letters have something in common.
    2. Consider looking up the dictionary definition of "vocalic."
    3. Vocalic means, "relating to or consisting of a vowel or vowels."
    4. There are only about a dozen words in the Scrabble dictionary that are Vocalic.
    5. A Vocalic word contains only vowels AEIOU.

    Monotonous

    1. In a Monotonous word, all of the letters have something in common.
    2. Any letter may appear in a Monotonous word.
    3. In a Monotonous word, all of the letters are the same letter.

    Sandwiched

    1. Try a few of the words from your known list of words.
    2. EYE is Sandwiched, and so is AHA.
    3. In a Sandwiched word, the first letter and the last letter are the same letter (but different from the middle letter).

    Primeval

    1. This one's tricky. It's hard to find even a single example to start with! But one of your known words is Primeval. (In the next hint, I'll tell you which one.)
    2. SKW is Difficult and Primeval. (See "Difficult" above.)
    3. All reorderings of SKW are also Primeval, like KWS and SWK.
    4. In a Primeval word, all of the letters have something in common. (All of the letters in a Primeval word are Primeval.)
    5. Starting with SKW, change just just one letter (e.g. just the first letter) to something else; try to find other Primeval letters. Working methodically, you can find more Primeval letters, and start to figure out what they have in common.
    6. The spellings of Primeval letters all have something in common.
    7. Remember how they spell letters here?
    8. Here, letters are spelled as spelled-out numbers, like one.
    9. The name "Primeval" is kind of a pun.
    10. The pun involves numbers.
    11. All of the Primeval letters are prime numbers, 2, 3, 5, 7, 11, 13, 17, 19, or 23. Primeval words contain only the letters BCEGKMQSW.
